## Rules
---

## 1. Eligibility
- Open to **all Stevens Institute of Technology students** (graduate & undergraduate).  
- Participants register individually but can form or join teams before hacking begins.  
- Teams can mix students from any school, program, or discipline.

---

## 2. Team Formation
- Form teams using **[Link](https://github.com/orgs/Hack-the-Clock/teams)**
- Teams can have **up to 3 members**.  
- Solo hackers are welcome too!  
- Each team must assign **one GitHub repo maintainer** for final submission.

---

## 3. Hacking Period
- Starts **9:00 AM** ‚Äì Ends **8:00 PM** on **Nov 6 2025**.  
- All work (code, design, model training) must be done **within the 12-hour window**.  
- Using open-source libraries, pre-trained models, or public APIs is allowed ‚Äî  
  but your **core logic** must be original and built during the hackathon.

---

## 4. Code of Conduct
We value inclusivity, respect, and collaboration.  
All participants must follow the **[MLH Code of Conduct](https://mlh.io/code-of-conduct)**.  

Harassment, discrimination, or misconduct will lead to immediate disqualification.  
Be kind, professional, and make it a great experience for everyone.

---

## 5. Originality & Fair Use
- Your submission must be **your team‚Äôs original work**.  
- You can use tutorials, snippets, and documentation ‚Äî but **no copy-paste projects**.  
- Credit all external assets, APIs, or datasets you include.

---

## 6. Disqualification
Disqualification may occur if a team:
- Uses pre-built or plagiarized solutions without disclosure  
- Submits after the deadline  
- Violates the Code of Conduct  
- Fails to include the required submission materials

---


Good luck and have fun hacking!
